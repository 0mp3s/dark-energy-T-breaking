I have taken this framework significantly further in two directions:

**Paper 1 — Full numerical SIDM scan:**
I implemented the full Variable Phase Method (VPM) for identical Majorana fermions with the proper spin-statistical weights (w_even=1, w_odd=3) and performed a 2.8-million-point scan over (m_χ, m_φ, α_s). The result: 80,142 viable points satisfying all 13 astrophysical SIDM constraints simultaneously, narrowed to 17 relic-constrained benchmarks with Ω_χh² = 0.120. A distinctive Majorana fingerprint emerges: σ_T^Maj/σ_T^Dir = 2.2 at v ≈ 60 km/s, with triplet-channel dominance at 82%. Code and data are published on Zenodo (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19225823).

**Paper 2 — Dark energy from the same Lagrangian (attached summary):**
I promote the CP phase to a dynamical dark axion σ, where σ is identified as a dark pion from SU(2)_d confinement with potential V(σ) = Λ_d⁴(1 − cos(σ/f)). The dark QCD confinement scale Λ_d ≈ 2 meV emerges naturally from dimensional transmutation with α_d ≈ 0.03 at μ = m_χ — producing the dark energy scale without fine-tuning the 10¹²² hierarchy.

The key result: solving a coupled σ–Friedmann ODE from reheating to today, H₀ emerges as output (not input) of the Lagrangian. Including the leading multi-instanton correction V(θ) = Λ_d⁴[(1-cosθ) + ε(1-cos2θ)] with ε ≈ -0.10 (natural for SU(2)_d), the model matches DESI DR2 (arXiv:2503.14738) at the 1.1–1.4σ level across all seven BAO redshift bins. The CPL parameters are w₀ ≈ -0.73 and w_a ≈ -0.49. Dark energy in this model is transient — it dies when σ oscillates to V = 0.
